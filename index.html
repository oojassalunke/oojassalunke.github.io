Using GANs to Generate New Images from Existing Datasets

In this project, we are utilizing Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) to generate new images while preserving the patterns of an existing dataset. GANs, a state-of-the-art algorithm for image generation, consist of two neural networks: a forger and a detective, working together to produce visually distinct yet statistically similar images.

Minimum Vertex Cover Algorithm Project

This project aimed to solve the NP-complete problem of minimum vertex cover using various approaches, including an exact method, an approximation algorithm, and two local search algorithms. The results indicated that while the exact method was more accurate for smaller and medium-sized graphs, it incurred higher computational costs. On the other hand, the approximation and local search algorithms provided reasonable solutions within a reasonable time, highlighting a trade-off between accuracy and computational budget.

Modeling Air Pollution Using Environmental Drones

This project explores the use of small drones to accurately measure air pollution levels in an urban environment, specifically focusing on Georgia Tech campus. By comparing the effectiveness of drone-based surveys with existing methods, such as spatial interpolation, the study analyzes flight paths, performance metrics, and cost considerations to provide a comprehensive solution for mapping and addressing air pollution concerns. The project demonstrates the successful implementation of a modeling and simulation-based approach to measure and mitigate air pollution levels at the university campus.
